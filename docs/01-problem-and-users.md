# 01 · Problem & Users

## The problem

Consumer AI in the "spiritual guidance" category has two chronic issues:

1. **Wall-of-text outputs.** Most apps paste a prompt into GPT and dump raw text on the user. No structure, no progressive disclosure, no shareability, no memory.
2. **Trust leakage.** Payment flows feel sketchy, refund terms hide, and there's no escalation when AI gets it wrong. Users disengage — or file chargebacks.

The target:

- Every AI output has a **structured shape** (summary → themes → elements → guidance) — skimmable, saveable, shareable
- **Payment and support feel legitimate** even when automated — because the business runs as a business
- History **preserved across modules** — tarot today, spell tomorrow, dream next week, one coherent thread

---

## Users

Three segments, prioritized by willingness-to-pay × engagement frequency:

| Segment | Need | Monetization |
|---|---|---|
| **Daily-ritual seekers** | Low-friction, high-frequency touchpoints | Subscription (Lucky Pro / Lucky Max) |
| **Depth-readers** | Structured, longer-form readings for life events | Credit packs (pay-per-reading) |
| **Skeptical first-timers** | Free entry point that doesn't feel predatory | Guest flow + email opt-in + first-reading gift |

---

## Why this category first

Not a believer story. A business-fit story:

- **Large, under-built.** Massive consumer demand. Almost no product thinking in the category.
- **AI-native.** Every module benefits from structured LLM outputs. No shoehorning.
- **Fast to validate.** Willingness-to-pay is high and measurable.
- **High governance surface.** Forces good habits — refund terms, chargeback defense, consent, RLS — skills that transfer to any regulated B2C product.

The category is the wrapper. The real project is a disciplined 0→1 AI consumer SaaS with strong unit economics and compliance posture.

---

## Non-goals

Explicitly not in scope:

- **No mobile app** — web-first shipped 10× faster
- **No social features** — viral loops would double scope; deferable
- **No custom models** — best-in-class APIs, swap per module as economics evolve
- **Not every module at launch** — 5 at v1.0, 7 more across v1.x → v2.x

Every "no" funded the "yes" list.
